13teen. A year of love, hate, and drama. Turning into a teenager feels like a big achievement. Indeed, I thought it was! Hight school years, new friends, new hobbies, new responsibilities, and of course new teenage kinds of stuff. It was the year 2019, I felt something really special about that year. And yes! I was finally 13Teen. Hot blood rushed throughout my body, an entirely different feeling, because I was no longer a kid now, I was a cool teenager and a high school student. Everything felt perfect. Watching myself in the mirror for about a hundred hours, using the best lip balm, brushing my silky hair for the millionth time, checking my teeth to be pearly white, and admiring the school uniform on me. I thought it was a new opening to endless dreams.

On the other hand, most of the elders told me that teenage is just a year of unwanted thoughts and overthinking, and they also added "use it wisely, cause you often tend to make fatuous decisions". Sounds weird, isn't it? Cause I thought it was. 13teen turned out to be exactly what I expected. I thought that people often made wrong predictions about teenage years. Cause mine was just perfect. I put all my time into sports and I got many new friends as well. Life was so amazing, every day I got up at 4:30, for my badminton practice, which was the best part of my day. Going to school immediately after badminton sessions were nightmares. But I did manage to, cause I just wanted to meet my friends every single day. To be honest, my friends were the best, they always remained to be by my side no matter what, they were very supportive and soon became just like my family.

When I had finally completed the last few months of 7th grade, I kind of started feeling completely different. I no longer felt like an excited kid anymore. Everything changed suddenly, for which I had no time to think and just move on. It all began with friendships, they no longer seemed so supportive and were always worried about their kinds of stuff. Soon it felt like we were all strangers with some memories. This, in the first place, was breaking my heart. I did not felt comfortable with them anymore, and I just felt like running away from all of these.

Maybe most of them were right, teenage life is just so difficult.
